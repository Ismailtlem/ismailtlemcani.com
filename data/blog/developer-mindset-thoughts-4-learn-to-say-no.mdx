This post is a powerful advice I got from a previous tech lead I had - one I believe every developer should remember.

As a developer, early in our career or early in a new job, we tend to say yes often. 
- Yes to every feature
- Yes to staying late to debug a bug 
- Can you develop this by tomorrow ? Yes. Even if you have to stay late at night

At first, this feels right. Saying yes shows motivation, commitment, and hard work. And yes this mindset helps you grow, especially early on.

However, it is not always the best thing to do as a professional. One of the most important quality you should have is to be reliable. If you say you'll do something, you should do it.
- Can you do this in 2 days ? If you see that 2 days is short, just say no and say that you need time to analyze the problem and to come up with a solution
- Can you fix this bug, that has been here for a long time, by tonight ? If you need time, just say no you can't.
- Can you become proficient in this technology by next week ? No. Learning takes time, and pretending otherwise helps no one.

Saying yes when you are unsure often leads to:
    - Missed deadlines
    - Poor-quality work
    - Stress and burnout
    - Loss of trust
Ironically, overcommitting makes you less reliable, not more.

## What to do then ?

Few thoughts to navigate those situations in your day to day job as a senior engineer :

- Replace “yes” with “let me assess it properly and get back to you with a realistic timeline.”

- Turn urgency into clarity: ask what is truly critical and what can wait.

Saying no is not about refusing work, it’s about setting clear expectations.
